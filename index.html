The Problem: Fragmented Playbooks

Department of War Program Offices, venture capital investors, and startups operate in isolation despite a shared desire to deliver warfighting capability faster than adversaries. This creates a systemic innovation-to-capability gap.

The Plan: A Three-Part Process

Alchemy envisions a system where the Department of War can see and shape emerging technology trends, where investors can identify the technologies most likely to transition, and where startups can find clear pathways to contribute to the mission. The long-term vision is a connected, data-driven marketplace where insight informs investment, investment drives innovation, and innovation reliably transitions into capability.
Insight Down Arrow

Create a data-driven common operating picture of technology areas that matter to both government and industry. Provide decision-makers with clear visibility into where defense demand meets commercial opportunity.

Investment Down Arrow

Incentivize performance by combining government demand signals and venture capital to accelerate promising technologies. Develop milestone-based models that reward measurable progress and signal private investment confidence.

Integration Down Arrow

Engage acquisition pathways from the start to ensure that successful prototypes can scale to programs of record. Reduce perceived risk by quantifying market strength and technology maturity.
